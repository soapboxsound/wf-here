Working in New York

New York has always been a city of people working in public. The café laptop crowd is not a recent phenomenon — it is how this city has always operated. What has changed is what people need: fast enough wifi for video calls, enough outlets to stay all day, and a noise level that does not require noise-cancelling headphones at full blast.

The best spots tend to cluster by type. Hotel lobbies in Midtown give you power, space, and anonymity — nobody asks how long you have been there. Cafés in the LES and Williamsburg have the wifi and the vibe but fill up fast after 10am. Libraries are the underrated choice: free, quiet, and the Rose Reading Room at NYPL is one of the most beautiful rooms in the city to open a laptop in.
